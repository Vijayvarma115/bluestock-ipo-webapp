Frequently Asked Questions

How to Subscribe to an IPO? +

Step 1: Log in to your respective service provider.
Step 2: Click on the IPO button.
Step 3: Select the IPO you want to bid on and enter the relevant details.
Step 4: Your subscription will be completed once you make the payment or give permission.

Should I buy an IPO on the first day? +

It depends on your investment strategy and the IPO details. Consider the risks and rewards before making a decision.

How do you know if an IPO is good? +

Research the company's financials, market position, and potential for growth. Consulting with financial advisors can also help.

How to check IPO start date? +

IPO start dates are usually announced by the stock exchanges or the company itself. Keep an eye on financial news and updates.

What is issue size? +

The issue size of an IPO refers to the total amount of shares or the total value of shares offered to the public by the company. It is calculated based on the number of shares and the price band set for the IPO.

How many shares are in a lot? +

The number of shares in a lot can vary from IPO to IPO. The company or the stock exchange defines the lot size, which indicates the minimum number of shares that can be applied for by an investor.

How is the lot size calculated? +

The lot size is calculated based on the minimum investment amount set by the company and the share price. It ensures affordability for retail investors and typically falls within a range defined by the stock exchange.

Who decides the IPO price band? +

The IPO price band is decided by the company in consultation with the book-running lead managers (BRLMs) or underwriters. It is based on the company’s valuation, market conditions, demand, and supply dynamics.

What is IPO GMP (Grey Market Premium)? +

IPO GMP refers to the premium amount at which IPO shares are traded in the grey market before they are listed on the stock exchanges. It provides an unofficial indication of the expected listing price.

How many lots should I apply for in an IPO? +

The number of lots you should apply for depends on your investment capacity, risk appetite, and the IPO’s potential. Consider diversifying your investments and consult with a financial advisor to make an informed decision.
